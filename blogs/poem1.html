I accede to it

February 2026

Every season passes, notions depart.
It is reality, the order of cruelty.
Maybe it was never meant to be; maybe it was just the heart chasing the wind.
Even nature sheds, cries, and mourns—who am I?
An outcast in a fleeting existence.
I accede to it.

Can I question? What should I question?
The delusion I lived in is more perpetual than the so-called eternal.
Is it foolishness or the inevitable that I chased you?
Would I change? Would my longings cease to exist?
My own soul cannot comprehend.
I accede to it.

Is it a sin if a trespasser longs for something not his own?
Getting stuck in a loop of illusion, a mirage in the desert of this heart.
An eternal illusion of the unattainable.
All the knowledge of this stranger is useless
Unless he's able to escape this wretched illusion.
I accede to it.

It was always you; everything was about you.
Now all I'm left with is a sparse reminiscence of you.
All I desire and hope for is quietness and tranquility.
All this cruelty and wretchedness has made me realize the true essence of life.
The greatest blessing a trespasser could hope for is stillness with his Maker.
I accede to him.
